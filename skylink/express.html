Express Entry is an online system used for skilled workers toapply for their permanent residence.This is the most popular and efficient way for candidates to obtain their permanent residence. This system is a competitive immigration system, ranking all eligible candidates against one another and then inviting the best candidates (according to the ranking) to apply for Canadian permanent residence. Candidates are ranked against one another based on some factors such as – age, education, language proficiency, work experience, and other factors. Candidates also required Language test results, education assessments, medical exams, proof of funds, job offers and police certificates.

Below – Mentioned are the three economic immigration programs under Express Entry:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program
  • Canadian Experience Class Program
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program



Requirements for Express Entry Express Entry Canada eligibility is determined using a point-based system called the (CRS) Express Entry Comprehensive Ranking System. The Canada Express Entry identifies potential immigrants that are likely to succeed economically once living in Canada. Express Entry CRS awards points for factors that Citizenship and CIC have found to correlate positively with future economic success for new immigrants to Canada. These Canadian Express Entry eligibility factors include some points such as – age, level of education, language proficiency and work experience in Canada.

Canadian Experience Class

Canadian Experience Class is for skilled workers that allows individuals who have worked in Canada for at least one year to immigrate permanently. Canadian Experience Class is part of Canada’s Express Entry immigration system and uses the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) to rank interested candidates.



Eligibility for Canadian Experience Class Program:

The candidate must have at least 12 months of full-time work experience or an equal amount of part-time work experience in Canada in order to be eligible for CEC.

The Canadian work experience must have been acquired within the previous three years from the application date.

There is no education requirement for the Canadian Experience Class.

The Candidate must meet the minimum language requirements of CLB 7 for NOC 0 or A jobs and CLB 5 for NOC B jobs.

The candidate is not inadmissible to Canada on medical, criminal or security grounds.

The Canadian Experience Class is best option for individuals who wish to resettle in Canada. For qualifying candidates, it is an convenient and secure option for obtaining permanent residence.



Federal Skilled Trades Program (FST)

Federal Skilled Trades(FST) Program is an opportunity for candidates to apply for their permanent residence in Canada based on being qualified in a skilled trade. FST program uses the Comprehensive Ranking System to rank competitive candidates. The approximate processing time for these applications is six months.

Canada’s National Occupational Classification code defines skilled trades as the below-mentioned occupation groups:

  • Industrial, construction and electrical trades (Major Group 72)
  • Maintenance and equipment operation trades (Major Group 73)
  • Supervisors and technical jobs in natural resources, agriculture and related production (Major group 82)
  • Processing, utilities and manufacturing supervisors, and central control operators (Major Group 92)
  • Chefs and cooks (Minor Group 632)
  • Bakers and butchers (Minor Group 633)
