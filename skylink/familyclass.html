This program is designed to help individuals reunite with their families. Under this program Canadian citizens, permanent residents, and persons registered in Canada as an Indian under the Canadian Indian Act can sponsor certain relatives to come to Canada as permanent residents.

The sponsor must be at least 18 years of age. The sponsor can apply for their spouse, partner or dependent children, parents and grandparents, siblings, and in some cases other relatives. The sponsor must be able to support the individuals being sponsored financially and ensure they don’t need social assistance from the government.

Requirements to be a Sponsor:

  1. Applicant must be 18 years of age or older.
  2. Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or registered India.
  3. Sign an undertaking stating that that you will provide for the basic requirements of the sponsored person(s).
  4. Meet the LICO for the past 12 months, unless sponsoring a dependent child who has no dependent child, an adopted person under 18 years, or a spouse, common-law or conjugal partner who does not have any dependent children or who does have dependent children who have no dependent child.
  5. For sponsoring Parents or Grandparents, meet LICO + 30% for the previous 3 taxation years.



Restriction for Sponsor:

  1. Receiving social assistance for a reason other than disability.
  2. Bankruptcy
  3. Subject to removal order
  4. Criminal History
  5. If the sponsor did not include the relative they want to sponsor on their own PR application.
  6. It has been less than 3 years since they previously sponsored a spouse, common-law partner, or conjugal partner.
  7. It has been less than 5 years since they became a PR by being sponsored as a spouse, common-law, or conjugal partner.
